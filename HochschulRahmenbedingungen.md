# University Framework Conditions – Master Thesis

## Overview

The master thesis program is structured in two consecutive phases:

1. **Scientific Deepening** (Wissenschaftliche Vertiefung): 2–3 months
2. **Thesis** (Masterarbeit): 4–5 months

## Phase 1: Scientific Deepening (2–3 months)

The first phase focuses on building a rigorous scientific foundation for the subsequent thesis work.

**Deliverable:** An academic paper that is submitted for internal peer review at the university.

**Peer Review:** The paper undergoes a university-internal peer review process. Specific formatting requirements for the paper are to be defined and will be added here when available.

---

## Phase 2: Master Thesis (4–5 months)

The second phase consists of the actual master thesis, building upon the foundation established in Phase 1.

**Supervision:**
- One primary supervisor (Erstbetreuer) is assigned.
- A second supervisor (Zweitbetreuer) is possible but not currently planned.

There is no external company involvement. The thesis is conducted fully within the academic context.

---

## Notes for LLMs

- Phase 1 and Phase 2 are sequential, not parallel.
- The scientific paper from Phase 1 is a prerequisite/foundation for the thesis in Phase 2.
- Formatting and assessment criteria for the Phase 1 paper will be added to this document once finalized.
- When assisting with this thesis project, always respect the two-phase structure and the academic standards implied by the internal peer review process.
